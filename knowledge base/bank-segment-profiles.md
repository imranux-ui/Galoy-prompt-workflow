# Bank Segment Profiles

*Reference for Prompt 04 outreach personalization. Match the target bank to a segment before running the email prompt. Each segment has different motivations, risk tolerances, and relevant Galoy products.*

---

## Community Bank
**Asset size:** Under $1 billion  
**Charter:** State or national; often FDIC-supervised  
**Typical structure:** Locally owned; board-driven; relationship banking model  

**What they care about:** Staying relevant to younger depositors, fee income in a compressed margin environment, not falling behind regional peers on digital offerings.

**What they don't want:** Regulatory risk, anything that looks like speculation to their board, complexity their small IT team can't manage.

**How to frame Galoy:** New fee income from Bitcoin brokerage without custody liability. Keep the compliance story front and center — they need to be able to explain it to a conservative board.

**Best product fit:** Bitcoin brokerage  
**Outreach hook:** Competitive pressure from larger banks and fintech — peer banks in their region are starting to offer this.

---

## Credit Union
**Asset size:** $100M to $5 billion  
**Charter:** NCUA-supervised; member-owned structure  
**Typical structure:** Board elected by members; mission-driven; often serving a specific community or employer group  

**What they care about:** Member service, financial inclusion, and demonstrating value to younger members who are increasingly asking about digital assets.

**What they don't want:** Anything that looks like speculation or that creates member harm risk. NCUA guidance on digital assets is their primary compliance reference.

**How to frame Galoy:** Member demand for Bitcoin access is real; the question is whether the credit union facilitates it or loses those members to Coinbase. Custody-independent structure limits the credit union's liability.

**Best product fit:** Bitcoin brokerage  
**Regulatory reference:** NCUA Letter to Credit Unions 22-CU-02 (digital assets); any subsequent NCUA guidance.

---

## Regional Bank
**Asset size:** $1 billion to $50 billion  
**Charter:** National (OCC) or state; may be publicly traded  
**Typical structure:** Multiple branches, dedicated digital banking team, compliance infrastructure  

**What they care about:** Competitive differentiation, digital revenue, retaining commercial clients who are increasingly holding or transacting in digital assets.

**What they don't want:** Regulatory enforcement action, custody risk, reputational exposure from a product that goes wrong publicly.

**How to frame Galoy:** Full product suite is relevant here. Bitcoin-backed lending fits naturally into commercial lending operations. Stablecoin payments are relevant for commercial clients with cross-border exposure. Bitcoin brokerage for retail.

**Best product fit:** Bitcoin-backed lending or stablecoin payments (commercial focus); brokerage (retail)  
**Outreach hook:** OCC and FDIC regulatory clarity is improving; the window to build the infrastructure before competitors is narrowing.

---

## Large National Bank / Money Center Bank
**Asset size:** Over $50 billion  
**Charter:** National (OCC); Fed-supervised if holding company  
**Typical structure:** Dedicated fintech partnership teams, significant internal compliance and legal resources, multiple regulatory relationships  

**What they care about:** Scale, regulatory standing, strategic positioning. They build most things internally or via acquisition; partnerships need to be clearly additive and low-risk.

**What they don't want:** A vendor that creates regulatory complexity or public exposure.

**How to frame Galoy:** Open-source infrastructure; integration-first; doesn't require custody transfer. Position as a specialist infrastructure layer for a specific product line, not a broad platform play.

**Best product fit:** Case-by-case; likely stablecoin payments or custody-independent lending infrastructure  
**Note:** Outreach to this segment typically requires a warm introduction. Cold email success rate is low. Better to target fintech partnership teams at conferences.

---

## Neobank / Digital-First Bank
**Asset size:** Varies; often deposit-light  
**Charter:** Often operates via bank partner (BaaS model); some have obtained independent charters  
**Typical structure:** Tech-forward, small team, moves fast  

**What they care about:** Product velocity, differentiation, user acquisition in a crowded market.

**How to frame Galoy:** Move fast; open-source; custody-independent means they're not stuck with one custodian. Technical team will appreciate the GitHub-first approach.

**Best product fit:** Bitcoin brokerage, stablecoin payments  
**Outreach tone:** More direct and technical than with traditional bank contacts. Developer framing is acceptable here.
