# Banned Phrases

*Content quality filter. These words and phrases are prohibited across all AI-generated outputs in this workflow — newsletter sections, LinkedIn posts, outreach emails, and internal documents.*

*Why this file exists: AI models default to high-probability tokens. In financial and marketing content, those tokens are consistently the same overused phrases. This list forces the model past its first-choice vocabulary.*

---

## Prohibited Words (standalone)

- landscape
- ecosystem
- robust
- seamless
- transformative
- innovative / innovation (unless describing a specific, named thing)
- leverage (as a verb meaning "use")
- utilize (use "use")
- empower / empowering
- solutions (as a standalone noun — "Galoy's solutions" → say what the thing actually is)
- synergies
- holistic
- cutting-edge
- state-of-the-art
- best-in-class
- world-class
- scalable (unless in a technical context with a specific meaning)
- streamlined / streamline
- frictionless
- disruptive / disruption
- pivotal
- unprecedented (almost nothing is)

---

## Prohibited Phrases

- "In today's rapidly evolving..."
- "In a significant development..."
- "In the ever-changing..."
- "As the digital asset landscape continues to..."
- "At the intersection of..."
- "game-changer" / "game-changing"
- "It goes without saying..."
- "It's worth noting that..."
- "Needless to say..."
- "This is just the beginning"
- "The future of banking is..."
- "We're excited to share..."
- "I'm thrilled / honored / proud to..."
- "Let's connect!" (email or LinkedIn sign-offs)
- "Drop a comment below"
- "What do you think?"
- "I hope this email finds you well"
- "I wanted to reach out because..."
- "Touch base"
- "Circle back"
- "Move the needle"
- "Low-hanging fruit"
- "Boil the ocean"
- "At the end of the day"
- "Think outside the box"
- "Hit the ground running"

---

## Phrases Requiring Caution

Use these only when they're genuinely the most precise option — not as defaults:

- "navigate" (overused in regulatory context — "comply with," "respond to," or "address" is usually more specific)
- "framework" (fine, but often used vaguely — specify what framework when possible)
- "space" (as in "the Bitcoin space" — say what you mean: "Bitcoin banking," "digital asset custody," etc.)
- "opportunity" (usually better to name the specific thing)
- "stakeholders" (usually better to name the actual parties: banks, regulators, customers)

---

## Formatting Prohibitions

These apply to all content regardless of platform:

- No emoji used as bullet points (✅ ➡️ 🔥 at the start of every line)
- No bold random phrases for emphasis in emails or social posts
- No markdown headers in email body text
- No hashtag stacks (one hashtag maximum on LinkedIn; zero on email)
- No exclamation points in outreach emails (maximum one per LinkedIn post, if necessary)
