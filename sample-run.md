## Prompt 02 Output — Newsletter Section

The OCC's April 2026 Interpretive Letter 1183 does something the industry has been waiting for since 2020: it gives national banks explicit written authority to custody Bitcoin under their existing fiduciary frameworks. No new charter application. No special-purpose license. The OCC is saying the existing rulebook already covers this — banks just needed the letter.

The practical effect is significant for how banks have been managing the "wait and see" question internally. Compliance teams that previously couldn't get a clean answer from their primary regulator now have one. That changes the internal conversation from "can we?" to "should we, and when?"

For banks that have been running informal feasibility work on digital asset products, this letter is the trigger to move that work into formal project planning. The banks that complete that planning first will have a meaningful head start on custody, brokerage, and lending infrastructure before peer institutions catch up.

## Notes on This Run

**What worked well:**
- The Radar entry's `One-Line Summary` translated cleanly into the newsletter opening without rewriting
- The LinkedIn post stayed under 120 words and landed a clear declarative close
- The email's 20-minute ask doesn't over-commit for a first touch

**What needed editing:**
- Prompt 02 initially ran to 215 words — trimmed the second paragraph to reach the 200-word ceiling
- Prompt 04 output assumed "Lakeside Commercial" was a national charter bank; confirmed before sending
- The LinkedIn hashtag felt natural here given the explicit regulatory news; would omit on a more opinion-driven post

**Total time:** ~18 minutes from input to four edited outputs
